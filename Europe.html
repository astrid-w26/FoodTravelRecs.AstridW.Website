United Kingdom:

I took a magical trip to the United Kingdom, where the air itself seemed to hum with quiet enchantment. In London, the streets shimmered after the rain, and every corner felt like it was hiding a secret waiting to be discovered. I wandered past glowing lamplight and ancient stone, half expecting time to slip and reveal another century beneath my feet. It felt like nothing and everything all at once, like I had stepped into a dream I didn’t want to wake from.


Food in London:

Breakfast

A visit to Regency Cafe is like stepping back in time to a classic London experience, full of charm and character. With its no-frills, retro atmosphere and friendly buzz, it has a cozy, welcoming feel that makes it instantly enjoyable. The food is hearty, comforting, and absolutely delicious—perfect for a satisfying lunch that hits the spot. Loved by locals and visitors alike, it’s a great place for all ages to enjoy a true taste of traditional British café culture.

Lunch

Tucked away in the heart of London, Sketch is a wonderfully fun and whimsical spot that feels more like stepping into an art installation than a café. With its playful interiors, colorful design, and imaginative atmosphere, it’s a place that sparks curiosity and delight for visitors of all ages. The lunch menu is just as impressive—delicious, beautifully presented, and perfect for a relaxed yet memorable midday treat. Whether you’re catching up with friends or bringing family along, Sketch offers an experience that’s as tasty as it is enchanting.

Dinner

I recently dined at Firebird in Soho and was seriously impressed by the whole experience. The atmosphere is warm and stylish without feeling pretentious, and the open-fire cooking gives the dishes a rich, smoky depth that really stands out. Every plate felt creative and beautifully balanced, especially the small sharing dishes and standout halloumi starter that so many people rave about. Service was attentive and relaxed, making it the kind of restaurant you immediately want to come back to.
